Airspace

ASSelect generates up-to-date airspace data for mainland UK in OpenAir format.

I try to make this data accurate, but there will still be some errors. Please don't blame me if you go somewhere you shouldn't have gone while using this data!

Output Format

The "RA(T) only" option downloads only the selected RATS. This gives you the option of updating your moving map software without changing the main airspace file.

"Competition" format is intended for competition organiser use only.

Airspace Types

Unlicensed Airfields are aerodromes (without an ATZ) listed in section ENR 5.5 of the AIP. The AIP does not include the majority of smaller airfields shown on the 1:500000 chart and they are not included by ASSelect.

Gliding Airfields are listed in ENR 5.5. ASSelect plots them with a radius of 1 nm. To avoid unnecessary airspace warnings ASSelect can exclude your home site.

Microlight Airfields are listed in ENR 5.5 and plotted with a radius of 0.5 nm.

Obstacles are listed in ENR 5.4. ASSelect includes only obstacles with a height of greater than 600 ft (mainly radio masts).

ATZs. A radio call is required before entering an active ATZ. The BGA recommend setting them to class D as a reminder. Setting them as Control Zones instead will also generate an airspace warning and also distinguishing them from true class D airspace.

ILS Feathers are not strictly an airspace type but the BGA recommend a radio call if you fly in their vicinity. Note the feather is symbolic only - the instrument approach procedure can extend either side of the feather.

Local Agreements

BGA letters of agreement are listed on the BGA website. With the exception of the Cambridge RAZ a local club briefing is required before using any local agreement.

Cambridge RAZ. The Cambridge Radio Advisory Zone (RAZ) is not controlled airspace. You are encouraged to make contact with Cambridge Approach before entering the RAZ. Unlike other LOAs the RAZ is permanently enabled in ASSelect. Note - Openair doesn't support a RAZ airspace type so a Radio Mandatory Zone (RMZ) type is substituted.

Radio Frequencies

ASSelect can optionally append radio frequencies to the airspace name.

Temporary Restrictions, RA(T)

RA(T)s are detailed in Mauve AICs on the NATS AIS website . RA(T)s can be downloaded separately from the rest of the airspace, see Output Formats above.

Data

Airspace data is updated every four weeks - see Schedule . I normally update this site approximately two weeks in advance of the effective date.
