Airspace

ASSelect generates airspace data for mainland UK in OpenAir format.

I try to make this data as accurate as possible, but there will still be errors. Navigation is your responsibility, not mine. So don't blame me if you go somewhere you shouldn't have gone while using this data!

Output Format

The output format is OpenAir.

The "OpenAir, RA(T) only" format downloads only the selected RATS. This lets you update your moving map software without needing to change the main airspace file.

Competition format is for competition organisers only.

Airspace Types

Unlicensed Airfields are aerodromes (without an ATZ) listed in section ENR 5.5 of the AIP. The AIP does not include the majority of smaller airfields shown on the 1:500000 chart and so they are not included by ASSelect.

Gliding Airfields are listed in ENR 5.5. ASSelect plots them with a radius of 1 nm. To avoid unnecessary airspace warnings ASSelect can exclude your home site.

Microlight Airfields are listed in ENR 5.5 and plotted with a radius of 0.5 nm by ASSelect.

Obstacles are listed in ENR 5.4. ASSelect includes only obstacles with a height of greater than 600 ft (mainly radio masts).

ATZs. A radio call is required before entering an active ATZ. The BGA recommend setting them to Class D as a reminder. However setting them as Control Zones instead will also generate an airspace warning while distinguishing them from true Class D airspace.

ILS Feathers are not strictly an airspace type but the BGA recommend a radio call if you fly in the vicinity. It's also recommended to set the airspace type to generate a warning on your moving map. Note the feather is symbolic only - the instrument approach procedure extends well outside the area of the feather.

Local Agreements

BGA letters of agreement are listed on the BGA website. With the exception of the Cambridge RAZ a local club briefing is required before using any local agreement.

Cambridge RAZ. The Cambridge Radio Advisory Zone (RAZ) is not controlled airspace. You are encouraged to make contact with Cambridge Approach before entering the RAZ. Unlike other LOAs the RAZ is permanently enabled in ASSelect. Note - Openair doesn't support a RAZ airspace type so a Radio Mandatory Zone (RMZ) type is substituted.

Radio Frequencies

ASSelect optionally appends radio frequencies to the airspace name.

Temporary Restrictions, RA(T)

RA(T)s are detailed in Mauve AICs on the NATS AIS website . RA(T)s can be downloaded separately from the rest of the airspace, see Output Formats above.

Data

Airspace data is updated every four weeks - see Schedule . I normally update this site approximately two weeks in advance of the effective date.
